Data Processing Agreement

Effective Date: June 18, 2026  ·  loadledgers.com  ·  Operated by dbappsystems.com

Section 1 — Purpose and Parties

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between the registered User of the Load Ledger Service ("Controller") and dbappsystems.com ("Processor"), and governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Load Ledger platform at loadledgers.com.

This DPA is incorporated by reference into the Load Ledger Terms of Service and Privacy Policy and shall be construed as part of the overall agreement between the parties.

Section 2 — Definitions

For the purposes of this DPA, the following definitions apply:

  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined under applicable United States data protection law
  • "Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion
  • "Controller" means the Load Ledger User who determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data through the Service
  • "Processor" means dbappsystems.com, which processes Personal Data on behalf of and under the instruction of the Controller
  • "Data Subject" means the natural person to whom Personal Data relates

Section 3 — Scope of Processing

The Processor shall process Personal Data only as necessary to fulfill its obligations under the Terms of Service and only in accordance with the documented instructions of the Controller, as reflected by the Controller's use of the Service.

The categories of Personal Data processed under this DPA include business contact information, load and dispatch records, billing and financial records, document uploads, and account activity data generated through use of the Service.

Section 4 — Processor Obligations

dbappsystems.com agrees to:

  • Process Personal Data solely in accordance with the Controller's instructions and the terms of this DPA
  • Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction
  • Ensure that all personnel authorized to access Personal Data are bound by enforceable confidentiality obligations
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay, and in no event later than seventy-two (72) hours, upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting Controller data
  • Provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in fulfilling Data Subject rights requests
  • Upon termination of the Service relationship, delete all Personal Data within thirty (30) days unless longer retention is required by applicable law

Section 5 — Controller Obligations

The Controller represents, warrants, and agrees to:

  • Ensure that a lawful basis exists for all Personal Data submitted to or processed through the Service
  • Provide accurate and complete information and maintain all necessary authorizations for data submitted to the Service
  • Comply with all applicable data protection laws governing the Controller's own use of the Service
  • Refrain from submitting Personal Data of third parties to the Service without proper legal authorization

Section 6 — Data Breach Notification

In the event of a confirmed Personal Data breach, the Processor will notify the Controller within seventy-two (72) hours of discovery. Such notice will include, to the extent known at the time: the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate volume of Personal Data affected, and the measures taken or proposed to address and mitigate the breach.

Section 7 — Data Subject Rights

The Processor will provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests for access, correction, deletion, restriction, or portability of Personal Data. All such requests should be submitted through the Customer Inquiry Form at https://loadledgers.com/contact. The Processor will respond within thirty (30) days of a properly submitted request.

Section 8 — Security Measures

The Processor maintains industry-standard security controls, including encryption of data in transit and at rest, access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel, and an incident response process for detecting and addressing security events. Security practices are reviewed and updated on an ongoing basis.

Section 9 — Confidentiality

The Processor shall treat all Controller data as strictly confidential. The Processor shall not disclose Controller data to any third party except as expressly permitted under this DPA or as required by applicable law. This obligation of confidentiality survives the termination of the Service relationship.

Section 10 — Term and Termination

This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the Controller's use of the Load Ledger Service. Upon termination of the Service relationship for any reason, the Processor shall delete all Personal Data within thirty (30) days, except as required by applicable law.

Section 11 — Severability

If any provision of this DPA is determined to be invalid or unenforceable, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

Section 12 — Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the United States. Where applicable data protection regulations require supplementary contractual provisions, the parties agree to execute such provisions as necessary to maintain compliance.

Section 13 — Contact

All inquiries related to this DPA, data rights requests, and breach notifications shall be submitted through the Customer Inquiry Form at: https://loadledgers.com/contact
